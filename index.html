Where engineering meets new complexity

I started coding during my time at Lindholmens Tekniska Gymnasium. I built things on the web and felt the pull of making something that actually works. But I kept running into the same problem: technically correct wasn't the same as useful.

That gap, between what a system does and what people actually need, became the thing I wanted to understand. So I moved into UX design, not away from engineering, but toward a different kind of problem-solving.

During my internship at Volvo Cars Data & AI Hub through Tekniksprånget, I worked with data systems and AI tools within Industrial Operations. That experience sharpened something: the harder it is for people to interpret information, the more important design decisions become. Trust isn't built through aesthetics alone. It's built through clarity.

I'm about to begin studying Chemical Engineering at KTH, a field that brings new kinds of complexity and new kinds of people to design for. The pattern I see is the same: understanding the system matters, but so does understanding the person standing in front of it.
